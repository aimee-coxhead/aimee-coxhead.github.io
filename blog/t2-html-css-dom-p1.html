HTML CSS and DOM Part 1

Can you think of an analogy to describe HTML, CSS and the DOM?
How would you describe it to your non-technical friend?

I would compare it to setting up a classroom.

HTML is your structure and content. This would be things like how many chairs and tables there are and where they are placed in your classroom. It would also be what information will be on your walls (e.g. a maths wall, reading wall ect) and were these walls are.

CSS is the style you would like. This would be what colour and shape you would like your tables to be. It would also be how the information on the different walls would be displayed (e.g fonts, spacing and background colours).

What is meant by boxifying design?

It is when you take every element on the webpage and divide it into boxes. A box can have other boxes inside it. For example, a header could have a box around it and then inside the header there could be a title which could have a box and then an image which could also have a box.

What is the box model?

Everything on a webpage is given a box. The box model shows how everything in the box relates/ is laid out. At the center of the box is the content. This could be your writing or image. The other layers go around the content. The next layer is the padding and this is the space around the content.The third layer is the border. The border goes around the content and the padding. The margin is the last layer and this is the space around the border.
