Motivation

How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion and purpose' lens?

Tony Hsieh describes three types of happiness. The first one is pleasure. Life through this lens would be about pursuing the next thing that makes you happy. The second type of happiness is passion. Life through this lens would mean that you are engaged in what you are doing and you are doing a good job at it. The last type of happiness is higher purpose. Life through this lens would mean that you are contributing to something that is bigger than yourself.

From the 'discovering values and higher purpose' exercise, What are your core values? What do you stand for?

My core value would be family. To have people around me that are loyal, dependable and who support me is something that I think is very important. I would stand for being true to yourself, being able to be your own individual and respecting what people have to say while also having the courage to stand up for yourself and your beliefs.

How did you find the resilience ideas and meditation exercise?

During the meditation exercise I found it difficult to experience the emotions without grasping or aversion. Reading through the Emotional Resilience section I think this is something that I will need to work on as I don’t won’t to let success or failure hold me back.

Any other musings?

“People want to be inspired!” Last year I had an experience with this in my job. I really wanted to implement something in our school that would benefit all of our our junior school students. By sharing this idea with everyone in my team, they were able to see how passionate I was about it and they all wanted to help out! In the end we were all able to work together for this greater goal. Sometimes we need to share our ideas with others so that you can achieve something that may not have been possible by yourself.
